Life Business Incubator — Launch. Build. Impact.

📘 Welcome to the Stark LBI Program

The Stark Life Business Incubator (LBI) is a 9-week program designed to nurture bold, Christ-centered founders as they build ventures that serve both purpose and profit. We help you launch with clarity, build with excellence, and scale with spiritual integrity.

Through structured weekly sprints, prayer sessions, funding access, mentorship, and a supportive founder community, LBI is where your faith meets your startup vision.

📏 Ground Rules & Discipline Policy

This is a Commitment to Purposeful Excellence.

✅ Attendance & Punctuality
  • All live sessions (sprints, prayers, reviews) are mandatory for each founder of a LBI business.
  • Attendance is recorded for every session. View the live attendance sheet here.
  • A grace period of 5 minutes for Prayer Sessions and 15 minutes for Live Sessions is allowed for latecomers.
  • 1 absence from Live Sessions = automatic removal from the program.
  • 2 absences from Prayer Sessions = automatic removal from the program.
✅ Deadlines
  • Assignments and sprint deliverables must be submitted by Friday morning, before your one-on-one session with Abiodun Lawal that same week.
  • Late submissions reduce access to funding and exposure opportunities.
  • Submit through this link.
✅ Google Drive Artefacts
  • Create a dedicated Google Drive folder for each week of the programme. Every worksheet, canvas, recording, screenshot, and document you produce that week goes into that folder.
  • Name your folders clearly: LBI W1 – [Your Name], LBI W2 – [Your Name], etc.
  • Share the relevant week's folder link when submitting your weekly deliverables form.
✅ Professional Conduct
  • Always use your full name when joining a live session or prayer meeting.
  • If possible, turn on your camera during live Zoom sessions.
  • Avoid distractions and multitasking.
  • Show respect to facilitators, mentors, and peers.

Got Questions?

Frequently Asked Questions

Getting Started
How do I know which programme is right for me?
The simplest test: do you have an idea or burden you feel compelled to build into a business? That's LBI. Do you feel more energised by coming alongside someone else's vision — executing, organising, and making things run, rather than leading the charge yourself? That's SOT.

Neither track is lesser than the other. SOT is not a fallback for people who could not get into LBI. If you are still unsure, email the team at theteam@heaveventures.com before committing.
Do LBI and SOT run at the same time? Will I attend all the same sessions?
Both programmes run during the same overall period (May 18 – July 17) but the SOT programme ends June 19 except for SOT Fellows merged with a LBI business.

LBI live sessions are Mondays, 6–8 PM WAT. SOT live sessions are Wednesdays, 6–8 PM WAT. Both tracks share prayer sessions on Tuesdays (11:30 AM) and Thursdays (7 AM).
I signed up but I have not received any onboarding information. What should I do?
Reach out to us via email at theteam@heaveventures.com. Include your full name and which track you are enrolled in (LBI or SOT).
Programme Structure
What exactly is the Merging Ceremony and do I have to participate?
The Merging Ceremony is on June 19 (Week 5). It is the moment LBI founders and SOT co-founders are formally matched, agreements are signed, and merged teams begin working as one unit.

If you're in LBI, you are expected to have a co-founder by this date. If your business already enrolled with a co-founder, merging with an SOT Fellow is optional. If you're in SOT, your entire 5 weeks build toward this moment.
What happens after the Merging Ceremony? Do the two cohorts now attend the same sessions?
From Week 6, if you merged with an LBI business, you join that founding team and work through the rest of the LBI curriculum together — operations, sales, finance, people management, and product readiness, all the way to Demo Day on July 17.

If you are an SOT Fellow who did not merge with an LBI business by the end of Week 5, your programme ends at that point.
SOT is only 5 weeks. What happens to SOT participants after the Merging Ceremony?
It depends. If you merge with an LBI business at the Merging Ceremony, you continue as part of that founding team through Demo Day (July 17). If you do not merge with an LBI business by the end of Week 5, you have completed your programme at that point.
What is Demo Day and who presents?
Demo Day is July 17, 2026 (Week 9) — the programme's closing event where founders pitch their startups publicly. By this point, Stark Fellows are expected to have a live product, paying customers, and a functional team. Every LBI founding team presents.
Weekly Tasks & Deliverables
How much time should I realistically set aside each week?
Treat STARK like a part-time commitment of roughly 8–12 hours per week, not a passive course you show up to. The workload increases as each week passes.

Each week involves at least one 2-hour live session, two 30-minute prayer sessions, plus time to complete that week's deliverables — tasks that can take several hours.
Where do I submit my weekly deliverables?
Deliverables are submitted via a Google Form linked in the Handbook section.

LBI: Due by Friday morning before your one-on-one session with Abiodun Lawal. Upload all artefacts to your weekly Google Drive folder first, then include the folder link in your submission.

SOT: Live sessions are Wednesday evenings. Deliverables are due by the following Tuesday evening. Upload artefacts to your weekly Google Drive folder and include the link in your submission form.
What is a deliverable, and is it graded?
Deliverables are tangible weekly outputs — a pitch recording, landing page URL, written statement, or worksheet. There is no formal grade but feedback can be given, and late or no submissions can lead to strikes. Think of them as proof of work.
Do I have to read the full books on the reading list?
No. The curriculum assigns specific chapters only (e.g. chapters 1–3) and encourages using AI tools (Google's Notebook LM) to summarise. Read what's listed and apply it to your startup context.
Attendance & Discipline
What if I have an emergency and can not attend a session?
Send an email to theteam@heaveventures.com or reach out to your WhatsApp group admin before the relevant session to be excused.
What's the difference between a strike and an absence?
An absence is a missed session and can trigger immediate removal. A strike is issued for conduct issues (disrespect, unprofessional behaviour, or no/late submissions).

LBI: 1st strike = warning, 2nd = dismissal. SOT: 1st = warning, 2nd = dismissal.
Can I catch up using session recordings?
Given the strict absence policy, treat every session as irreplaceable. Nonetheless, recordings may be made available subsequently.
How do I know if my attendance was recorded?
Each track has a live attendance sheet linked in its Bye-Laws page. For LBI, view it here.
Faith & Community
Do I need to belong to a specific denomination?
No. STARK is Christ-centred and integrates prayer and scripture throughout, but has a focus on individuals within the Word of Righteousness communities.
What is the WhatsApp community for?
It is the main space for cohort interaction between sessions — peer feedback, announcements, and support. If you haven't received an invite, contact the Stark team.
Is mentorship available?
Yes. Mentorship is a programme benefit for LBI Fellows, primarily accessed through office hours on Fridays and Saturdays. Bring specific questions or deliverable challenges.
